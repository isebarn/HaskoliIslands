[Tekið og stytt úr grein frá 2015]

N-acetylcysteine has been researched over the past decade and there is evidence for the use of NAC in treating psychiatric and neurological disorders, including oxidative stress, apoptosis, mitochondrial dysfunction, neuroinflammation and glutamate and dopamine dysregulation. 
Favorable evidence exists for the use of NAC in treating autism, Alzheimer's disease, cocaine and cannabis addiction, bipolar disorder, depression, trichotillomania, nail biting, skin picking, obsessive-compulsive disorder, schizophrenia, drug-induced neuropathy and progressive myoclonic epilepsy.
